Applebee's Grilled Chicken Oriental Salad

Salad Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breast halves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup sliced almonds
  • 6 to 8 cups romaine lettuce
  • red cabbage
  • green onion
  • 1/4 cup shredded carrots
  • 1/2 cup crispy rice noodles

Dressing Ingredients

  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons rice wine vinegar
  • 1/4 cup Mayonnaise
  • Sesame Seed Oil
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon Mustard

DIRECTIONS:

  • Heat the grill to medium, or heat a cast-iron skillet or grill pan over medium heat.
  • Cut chicken breast into 4 or 5 long strips each.
  • Option 1:  Brush with the olive oil and season them with salt and pepper. 
  • Option 2:  Flour, Egg, and Corn Flake Crumbs.
  • Grill for 5 to 7 minutes on each side, until cooked through.
  • Transfer to a plate to rest for 4 to 5 minute before slicing.
  • Toast the almonds in a small dry skillet over medium heat.   Watch them carefully—there is a fine line between toasted almonds and burnt almonds!   Shake the pan gently.   When you begin to smell the almonds, toast for a few seconds more, then immediately place the almonds on a paper towel.  Allow them to cool for a moment or two.
  • Assemble salads by first putting down the lettuce, 3 to 4 cups per serving.
  • Sprinkle each with 2 tablespoons of the carrots, 1/4 cup crispy rice noodles, and 1/4 cup toasted almonds.
  • Arrange the chicken on top. Serve with plenty of the Applebee’s Oriental Salad Dressing.

Directions

  • In a medium bowl combine rice vinegar and honey, mix until completely dissolved. 
  • Add the mayonnaise and the mustard and stir until well blended. 
  • Refrigerate the salad dressing in a covered container for up to 1 week.

Notes About the Ingredients

  • Rice vinegar is carried in most grocery stores, be careful not to purchase the seasoned rice wine vinegar, as it is sweetened and makes the dressing way too sweet.
  • The refined sesame oil can be found in the regular section of the oils in a grocery store. For the toasted sesame oil, look in the Asian section of your grocery store.
