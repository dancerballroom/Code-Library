You can also use ffmpeg to shift sub times.
ffmpeg -i in.srt -c copy -output_ts_offset 4.52 out.srt
All subtitle times will be delayed 4.52 seconds.
You can make them earlier, to bring forward 2.1 secs
ffmpeg -i in.srt -c copy -output_ts_offset -2.1 out.srt

However, when bringing them forward, ffmpeg will not produce negative timings.
So if in the above case, the first line starts at 1.5 secs, then the offset 
will be -1.5 i.e. first line starts at 0, and all other timings are decreased 
by 1.5.
