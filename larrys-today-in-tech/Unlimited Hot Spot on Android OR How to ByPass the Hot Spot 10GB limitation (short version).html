Unlimited Hot Spot on Android OR How to ByPass the Hot Spot 10GB limitation (short version)

Pre-requisite:  A data cable to connect the phone to the computer, which is similar to a charging cable.  NOTE:  You may need to turn on DEBUGGING on your phone if it asks.  If so, give me a call and I’ll walk you through it.  I don’t remember for sure if it asks but it’s easy to do.

Here goes:

    • NOTE the Unlimited LTE Data Used      (this month I used over 100GB)

  • Set Up your phone
    • Allow installing from unknown websites and folders
      • Swipe and click Settings/choose Biometrics and Security/choose Install Unknown apps
        • SET Chrome: Allowed
        • SET My Files: Allowed
    • Download and Install PDANet from the PLAY Store
    • Google Search Aptoid,  download and install Aptoid (Aptoid is an alternative to the Google Play Store.  It has advertising, but it has access to many more products, both good and questionable)
      • NOTE:  Any problems with starting the install, the APK should be in the download directory

    • Inside Aptoid, Then search for, download, and install “foxfi key”
    • Run foxfi and then Run “foxfi key” to register the foxfi.
    • Very Important: On your Cell phone Swipe and click SETTINGS/choose Apps/choose PdaNet+/choose Storage
    • Clear Data and Clear Cache
    • Open foxfi Key (It opens PDANet+)
    • On your phone, In PDANet, which you can open instead of foxfi from now on, click USB tether
    • Make sure your data cable is plugged into your phone
    • Done with CELL
  • Finally to get your computer to work properly download and install the Windows Installer for “PDANet” from http://pdanet.co/install
  • When you click the icon in the PdaNet tray icon, which may be hidden in the taskbar you can click “Connect to USB” and it will make the phone think your computer is a part of itself and use no hotspot data.   You can check this by repeating step 1 above to check your hotspot data use.
  • NOTE:  There is a setting where you can Automatically have the phone connect to the computer.  You can find the SETTINGS in the same menu where you see “Connect to USB”
  • NOTE:  The service is fairly good, but I do notice that the WiFi dies during high internet traffic, mainly in the mornings and the evenings and sometimes during foul weather.
