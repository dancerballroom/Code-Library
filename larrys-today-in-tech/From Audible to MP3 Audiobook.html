From Audible to MP3 Audiobook

Why save an Audible book as MP3?

My dad had over 50 books downloaded from Audible when he passed away.  As soon as he passed away and I used the Audible application with my credentials the access to his books was denied and his library was deleted on the local computer by iTunes, even though my father had paid $100s of dollars for his books.

Also, unless you download or stream and somehow convert the books, Audible decides how many devices you are allowed to listen to, and if you are on a trip and don’t have them already downloaded, they can’t be listened to.

You don’t OWN anything!  Life used to be different.  You may buy a book.  You may think you have something in your LIBRARY, and have the rights to it... YOU DON’T... but you do pay for it.

NOTE: Your PHONE and PC will be UNAVAILABLE FOR (Length of BOOK)

Example:  Bk 13 Ghost Story of the Dresden Series took 17 hours to Record

Physical Setup:

  • Buy the Kindle book on Audible.  I have the PC and the Android Version.
    • Note:  Only the Android Version supports the Genuine Audio Book
    • The PC only supports the Computer Generated Voice
  • Play Audible Audiobook through Kindle on PHONE
  • Plug “Speaker Out“ into ”Microphone IN“ on Computer
    • NOTE:  Several Years ago the Powers that be decided to disable REC apps on the phone from recording from the phone.  Supposedly this was to protect for Privacy issues.  Right!  (The phone still records around itself.)

Software Setup (Computer):

  • Open “Sound Settings” 
  • Choose “Input Device” MIC, “Listen to Device” Experimental (Works 1/2 time)
  • Choose “Output Device” Speaker / (Headphones)
  • Open Audacity
  • Monitor Input
  • Check Levels 
    • Check for Distortion (Too Loud)
    • Check for Too Quiet
    • Check for Sound Problems/Interference
  • Run Record / PlaybackTests
  • Audacity Prep:
    • Make sure MP3 export/conversion add-in is installed.
      • (Large FILES over 4GB WAV files will not save)

Phone Prep

    • Turn off Notifications from other apps
    • Turn off calls / messages
    • Set Keep Phone On
    • Plug In Charger
    • Cue up Book to Beginning, 
    • Plug in Phone Charger

Recording Process

    • Start Recording
    • Start Playing
    • Check Headphones
    • Check Progress in Kindle Audiobook App

Save Audiobook Process

    • Save as MP3
    • Edit MP3 to Add missing DATA or Append missing DATA (Can also use @Voice for Autonomy)
    • Migrate to Computer using USB Tether

Prepare for Listening by Music Folder App

    • Use MP3Tag to add Metadata
      • Image (Saved from Google Image Search) or (Screen Capture)
      • Series Name, Series Number, Book Name, Author, Book Image, Date Published, Comments
    • MP3Splitter to Chop into Multiple Parts
    • MP3Tag
      • Rename Files in Order (01 of 31 - Name - Bk 03 - Series Name - Author)
    • Archive Folder, Transfer to Phone and other Devices, Rescan

DONE!
